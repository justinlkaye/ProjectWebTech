Okemo Mountain Resort

  • Number of Trails: 121
  • Trail Mix: 32% Green, 38% Blue, 30% Black
  • Skiable Acres: 667
  • Lifts: 20

Okemo Mountain Resort is a premier ski destination located in Ludlow, Vermont. With over 120 trails, a wide range of terrain parks, and a variety of dining and lodging options, Okemo has something for everyone. The resort also offers a range of activities beyond skiing and snowboarding, including snowshoeing, ice skating, and tubing.

Tips:

  • Park in the Jackson Gore parking lot for the easiest access to the slopes.
  • Use the Sunburst Six lift for access to a variety of terrain, including some of the resort's best black diamond runs.
  • Check out the Solitude Day Lodge for a quieter dining option.

Park City Mountain Resort

  • Number of Trails: 348
  • Trail Mix: 8% Green, 42% Blue, 50% Black
  • Skiable Acres: 7,300
  • Lifts: 41

Park City Mountain Resort is one of the largest ski resorts in North America, located in Park City, Utah. With over 300 trails and seven terrain parks, Park City offers some of the best skiing and snowboarding in the world. The resort also offers a range of dining and lodging options, as well as a variety of activities beyond skiing and snowboarding, including snowshoeing, snowmobiling, and zip lining.

Tips:

  • Park in the Canyons Village parking lot for the easiest access to the slopes.
  • Use the Quicksilver Gondola for access to both Park City and Canyons Village areas of the resort.
  • Check out the Summit House for a great lunch spot with stunning views.

Stowe Mountain Resort

  • Number of Trails: 116
  • Trail Mix: 16% Green, 59% Blue, 25% Black
  • Skiable Acres: 485
  • Lifts: 13

Located in Stowe, Vermont, Stowe Mountain Resort is a premier ski and snowboarding destination. With 116 trails spread across 485 skiable acres, there is something for everyone. The trail mix is 16% beginner, 55% intermediate, and 29% advanced, so both novice and experienced skiers and snowboarders can enjoy the mountain. The resort also boasts 12 lifts, including a gondola, 3 high-speed quads, and a new six-pack, providing easy access to the slopes.

Tips:

  • Arrive early to get a good parking spot in the Mansfield lot, which is closest to the gondola.
  • Take the gondola to the top of Mount Mansfield for some of the best views on the mountain.
  • For a challenging run, try Liftline, a double-black diamond trail that runs from the top of the gondola down to the base.
